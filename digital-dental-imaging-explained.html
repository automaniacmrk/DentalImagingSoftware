Digital Dental Imaging Explained

In the past, dentists' offices had film-based radiography equipment that required a darkroom to process images. Many providers realized that the traditional method of using practice resources was inefficient and time-consuming with the advent digital imaging tools.

Practices that employ digital imaging don't need a darkroom at all. They can use sensors, pans or intraoral cameras instead. These images can be generated instantly and accessed via the computer without any chemical processing.

Here are some of the advantages of digital imaging as described by a study published in the Journal of the American Dental Association (JADA):

  • Instant image production
  • Display on interactive monitor with ability to magnify image details and take direct measurements
  • Referral purposes: Perfect radiographic duplicates
  • Security mechanisms to identify original images and differentiate them from altered images
  • The ability to tag information such as a patient identifier, date of exposure and other relevant details

It appears a great deal of practices are already hoping to capitalize on these benefits. We found that 18 percent of dentists cite digital sensor integration and imaging devices as the top-requested functionality in a Software Advice survey of software buyers trends.

A One-Study Diagnostic is Fast and Easy

Actual scan time is approximately 20 seconds for full mouth. Panoramic images provide more coverage for bone defects and jaw lesions.
