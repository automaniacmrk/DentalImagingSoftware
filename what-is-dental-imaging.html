What is Dental Imaging?

Care providers can't get an accurate picture of the patient's oral health without performing an oral exam. Dr. Dentists need to be able see any troubling conditions, including hidden decay or extra teeth. They also have to know if the patient is experiencing bone loss due gum disease.

That's where dental imaging technology comes in. It allows professionals to get more clear and comprehensive pictures of teeth, tissue, nerves and bone inside the mouth. These images can then be studied, stored and compared over time to track improvements or identify worsening conditions.

DentiMax Software displaying an X-ray image

Because dentists can detect problems early, dental imaging gets precise diagnoses. They can also share images with patients and point out specific problems. This allows them to educate their patients better.
